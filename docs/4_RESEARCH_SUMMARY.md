## Cross-Bank Structural Patterns

### 1. Monthly Caps Are Universal and Restrictive
Every cashback card verified has monthly caps, typically AED 150–500 per category. **No cashback card in the verified set exceeds AED 1,000/month in a single category.** This means:
- High spenders (AED 15K+/month) will hit caps early and need portfolio diversification
- Portfolio of 3–4 cards often beats any single card by 2–3x in annual value

### 2. Points vs. Cashback: Cashback Is More Transparent
Points-based cards (FAB Rewards, ENBD Skywards, Citi ThankYou, SC 360°, DIB Wala'a) all require knowing the redemption rate to calculate effective return. Cashback cards (FAB Cashback, Mashreq Cashback, Liv Cashback, HSBC Cash+, DIB Consumer Cashback) provide transparent AED value directly.

**Key verified point values:**
- FAB Rewards: AED 0.003/pt
- ENBD Skywards Miles: AED 0.0143/mile (approx. using typical Skywards redemption)
- Citi ThankYou Points: AED 0.033/pt (verified from T&C)
- SC 360° Rewards: AED 0.008/pt (125 pts = AED 1)
- DIB Wala'a Rewards: AED 0.005/pt (20,000 pts = AED 100)
- ADIB SHARE Points: AED 0.01/pt
- RAKBank Rewards: AED 0.01/pt

### 3. Forex Markup Is a Hidden Cost Many Users Underestimate
All verified cards except FAB Travel Card carry forex markups of 2–3.70%. On AED 20,000/month of foreign currency spend, this costs AED 400–740/month — often more than the card earns in rewards. **FAB Travel Card (0% forex) is the only verified card where international spending is unambiguously net-positive.**

### 4. UAE-Only Cards: A Significant Hidden Limitation
Several cards earn **zero** on international transactions, which is not prominently advertised:
- **ENBD LuLu 247:** Cashback on UAE spend only
- **Mashreq Cashback / Noon:** UAE spend only
- **DIB Consumer Cashback:** UAE transactions only (explicitly stated)
- **Liv Cashback / Cashback+:** UAE only (implied)
These cards are effectively useless for travelers or online purchases from foreign merchants.

### 5. Category Exclusions Are Inconsistent Across Banks
There is no standard across UAE banks for which categories earn:
- **Utilities:** Earns on ADIB (4%), DIB (4%), Wio (0.5%) — but 0% on most cashback cards
- **Government fees:** Earns on DIB (4% on Salik/NOL only), Wio (0.5%) — typically 0% elsewhere
- **Rent:** Typically 0% or general rate; RAKBank explicitly 0%; Wio 0.5%
- **Insurance:** Varies — Citi excludes insurance; most cards treat it as general spend

### 6. Minimum Spend Thresholds Are Common
Several cards require a minimum monthly spend to earn at the bonus rate:
- FAB Travel Card: AED 5,000 previous month
- FAB Cashback: AED 3,000 previous month
- DIB Consumer Cashback Platinum: AED 4,000/month
- DIB Consumer Cashback Reward: AED 3,000/month
- ENBD LuLu 247 Platinum: AED 3,000/month (for 7% rate)
Users who don't meet these thresholds may earn at a reduced or zero rate.

### 7. Islamic/Shariah Cards: Similar Earning, Key Exclusions
Verified Islamic cards (FAB Cashback Islamic, ADIB, DIB) follow similar earning structures to conventional counterparts but with:
- MCC-level blocking of haram merchants (alcohol, gambling, tobacco) — not visible to end user, handled by issuer
- DIB covered cards are Salam-based (purchase finance structure), not conventional credit
- No meaningful difference in effective_return_pct for Shariah-compliant cardholders in most categories

### 8. Multi-Tier Cards Discovered During Verification
Several "single cards" during initial data entry turned out to be separate tier products:
- **FAB Etihad Guest:** 3 tiers (Platinum, Signature, Infinite) — different earn rates
- **FAB SHARE:** 3 tiers (Rewards, Platinum, Signature) — different earn rates
- **Blue FAB:** 3 tiers (Platinum, Signature, Infinite) — replaced original FAB Infinite
- **ENBD LuLu 247:** 2 tiers (Platinum, Titanium) — different rates
- **ENBD Marriott Bonvoy:** 2 tiers (World Elite, World) — different earn rates
- **DIB Wala'a Infinite** replaced by **DIB Prime Platinum** (card no longer active)
**Learning:** Always read T&C for tier comparison tables before assuming one card = one product.

## Reward Program Structural Findings

### Cashback Programs (Direct AED)
FAB Cashback, Mashreq Cashback, Mashreq Noon, Liv Cashback, Liv Cashback+, ADIB Cashback, HSBC Cash+, HSBC Live+, SC Platinum X, DIB Consumer Cashback, Citi Cash Back — all pay directly in AED or as statement credit. Transparent, no redemption complexity.

### Miles Programs
- **ENBD Skywards:** Ties you to Emirates Airline; best value when redeemed on Emirates flights
- **FAB Etihad Guest:** Ties you to Etihad Airways; strong earn rate on Etihad spend (up to 2.75% Infinite tier)
- **SC Journey (360° Rewards):** Transferable to multiple partners; 0.44% on local spend (weak vs. cashback alternatives)

### Points Programs (Transferable)
- **Citi ThankYou Points:** 0.033 AED/pt; strongest value in the set; transfers to airlines
- **DIB Wala'a Rewards:** 0.005 AED/pt; redeemable for flights, hotels, bill pay; 24-month expiry
- **ADIB SHARE Points:** 0.01 AED/pt; 1.5% effective on general spend (Infinite tier)
- **FAB Rewards:** 0.003 AED/pt; lowest effective value per point; dependent on promotional redemptions

## Data Quality Observations

### Earn Rates Not in T&C PDFs
For some banks, earn rates are NOT stated in the T&C PDF and must be sourced from product pages:
- **DIB:** T&C explicitly states rates are "published on DIB website" — product pages used as verified source
- **ADCB:** Supplemental benefits T&C covers perks only; earn rates unavailable (adcb.com returns 403)
- **ADIB:** Earn rates found in a separate "Comparison of Card Offering" PDF, not the main T&C

### Forex Markup Corrections Made During Verification
Several cards in the initial scrape had wrong forex markups:
- HSBC cards: 2.99% → **2.0%** (corrected from hsbc.ae)
- DIB cards: 2.99% → **3.70%** (corrected from KFS PDF)
- ENBD: some cards corrected to 1.99%
**Implication:** Never trust the initial scraped forex_markup_pct; always verify from KFS PDF or bank website.

### Cards Replaced During Verification
- **SC Simply Cash** → replaced by **SC Journey** (Simply Cash discontinued for new applicants)
- **DIB Wala'a Infinite** → replaced by **DIB Prime Platinum** (Wala'a Infinite no longer on DIB website)
- **FAB Infinite** → replaced by **Blue FAB Platinum/Signature/Infinite** (rebranded)
**Implication:** Bank product lineups shift; always check if the card still exists before verifying.
